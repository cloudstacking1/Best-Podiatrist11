FAQ's

A podiatrist is a doctor specializing in diagnosing and treating conditions related to the feet, ankles, and lower legs. They manage issues ranging from sports injuries and fractures to chronic problems like plantar fasciitis and diabetic foot care. Podiatrists can provide non-surgical treatments and perform surgeries for severe conditions.

You should visit a podiatrist if you experience persistent foot or ankle pain, deformities like bunions or hammertoes, or issues such as ingrown toenails, fungal infections, or sprains. Seeking early care can prevent complications and provide relief through appropriate treatments.

Podiatrists frequently treat conditions such as heel pain (e.g., plantar fasciitis), flat feet, bunions, sprains, fractures, corns, calluses, and diabetic foot complications. They also assist athletes in addressing sports-related injuries and offer solutions like orthotics for support.
